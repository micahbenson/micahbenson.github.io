I am a 4th year undergraduate at Washington University in St. Louis studying data science and English. I am passionate about advancing AI systems by reducing social bias in their outputs. I am especially interested in natural language processing and exploring interactions between humans and AI systems. The goal of my reserach is to ensure that AI benefits society instead of perpetuating injustices. 

I currently work as a reserach assistant at the Zhang Translational Genomics Lab in the Department of Radiation Oncology at WashU's School of Medicine. I develop machine learning models to predict the recurrance of cervical cancer in patients, with the goal of designing models that can one day be used to improve patient outcomes by informing more precise treatment plans.

I also work as a data analyst intern at the Misíon de Caridad, a nonprofit that empowers migrants in the Mexican border town of San Luis Río Colorado to live free of poverty through programs that provide education, employment, healthy food and clean water, and medical resources to the community. 

In my free time I love to explore nature by running, hiking, and biking. I am a member of the varsity cross country and track & field teams at WashU.
